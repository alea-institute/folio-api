  <action>
Add performance.mark calls at four key boundaries in the existing pipeline:

1. At the start of refreshFor (after dedupe early-return):
       performance.mark('eg:fetch:start');

2. After fetch().then(res => res.json()) resolves (before buildELKGraph):
       performance.mark('eg:fetch:end');
       performance.measure('eg:fetch', 'eg:fetch:start', 'eg:fetch:end');
       performance.mark('eg:layout:start');

3. After runLayout's promise resolves (after laid is assigned to state.graphData.layout):
       performance.mark('eg:layout:end');
       performance.measure('eg:layout', 'eg:layout:start', 'eg:layout:end');
       performance.mark('eg:render:start');

4. At the end of _mountGraph (after the SVG + nodes are appended, after fitGraph is queued):
       performance.mark('eg:render:end');
       performance.measure('eg:render', 'eg:render:start', 'eg:render:end');
       performance.measure('eg:total', 'eg:fetch:start', 'eg:render:end');

Wrap each performance call in `if (typeof performance !== 'undefined' && performance.mark)` to be defensive (some test environments stub performance).

Notes:
- Marks are zero-cost when not actively measured.
- The DevTools Performance tab shows the user-timing track with these labels grouped under "Entity Graph".
- After implementation, do NOT remove the marks — they stay in production code as a permanent observability hook.
  </action>
